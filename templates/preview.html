You asked

What does Borges say about the nature of labyrinths and infinity?

Answer

For Borges, the labyrinth is not merely an architectural puzzle but a metaphysical condition. He treats it as the natural shape of the universe itself — infinite, recursive, and ultimately indifferent to the creature lost within it. In both Labyrinths and Ficciones, he returns again and again to the idea that all labyrinths conceal something monstrous at their center: sometimes a Minotaur, sometimes a god, sometimes nothing at all — and that the nothing is perhaps the most terrifying possibility.
